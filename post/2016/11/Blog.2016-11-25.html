Unseen

Written by Dallas Pepper on November 25th, 2016.

"What don't you see?"
That was the question God asked me as I looked at my arms like He had asked. The answer was scars. Where scars should be my skin is smooth.
"Sometimes" He said "My grace is what you don't see."
I didn't deserve to be healed so fully. I didn't deserve anything other than death. The marks that use to be on my arms were my own doing. God didn't have to but He did. And I hadn't even noticed. I stopped to think about all the gifts God has given that I don't even see. How many consequences for my own actions have I not endured? How many car wrecks have I not been in? How many illness have I not had? How many schemes and weapons have been formed against me that were broken before they touched me? I was reminded that God is not distant and inactive. He watches me like a parent watches an infant or toddler, with constant vigilance. That is certainly something to be thankful for.

About

This started as a series of emails to a small group of my sweet sisters and quickly grew. I heard a pastor once say that he was preaching to himself. We were just listening in to what God had been working personally in him. That is what this blog is. It is a window into what God has been working in me. I pray this will help encourage other believers as they run their own race.
