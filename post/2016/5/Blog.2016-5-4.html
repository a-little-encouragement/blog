Crucibles and Security Blankets

Written by Dallas Pepper on May 4th, 2016.

"Sooner or later, we all go through a crucible...Most believe there are two types of people who go into a crucible. The ones who become stronger from the experience and survive it, and the ones who die. But there's a third type. The ones who learn to love the fire and choose to stay in their crucible because it's easier to embrace the pain when it's all you know anymore."
- Sebastian Blood : Arrow season 2 episode 4 "Crucible"


I'll confess I've been the third type. Pain and depression are probably the most familiar things to me. This is quickly becoming untrue. But I still struggle with the tendency to grab onto it like Linus with his blanket. It clouds my eyes. It blinds me to God's presence and makes Him feel far away. Even Linus dropped his blanket when focused on Jesus. I want to trade in my blanket for the joy of the Lord. Angela Thomas talked about this principle in her study Stronger. She compared it to the author of Psalm 84 traveling through the Valley of Baca(weeping). They had to go through it, not make their home there. While there, when they get thirsty, they dig. We too should dig for the living water that Jesus promises. Press in. Spend more time in prayer, spend more time worshiping, spend more time in the Word, but don't stop digging. Jesus promises to draw near to us if we draw near to him.
