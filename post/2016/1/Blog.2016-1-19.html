On The Barely Fields Of Bethlehem

Written by Dallas Pepper on January 20st, 2016.

So to continue my story, the match of me and this man just seemed wrong and unfair. I was a wandering widow and he was from a rich family firmly established in the community. Story sound familiar? My identity crisis brought me to the barely fields of Bethlehem. I wouldn't have expected the story of Ruth to make to feel better. But as I studied the book two things struck me. The first was the way Boaz speaks to/of Ruth. The word he uses to describe Ruth is also in Proverbs 31. He clearly didn't think he was getting a bad deal. The second thing I noticed was even though they were very different physically speaking, spiritually they were cut from the same cloth. There is a lot I could say of the book of Ruth but I won't try to in this post. God used that to help me overcome my false sense of identity. It is still a struggle sometimes, but I remind myself I'm not who I was. God has washed me and made me clean. I am a beloved daughter of the most high King. I am His and He is mine. I have another song because sometimes He just likes to dance with his girls.

Verse:

11 But Boaz answered her, “All that you have done for your mother-in-law since the death of your husband has been fully told to me, and how you left your father and mother and your native land and came to a people that you did not know before. 12 The Lord repay you for what you have done, and a full reward be given you by the Lord, the God of Israel, under whose wings you have come to take refuge!” - Ruth 2:11-12

11 And now, my daughter, do not fear. I will do for you all that you request, for all the people of my town know that you are a virtuous woman. - Ruth 3:11

Who can find a virtuous[a] wife? For her worth is far above rubies. - Proverbs 31:10

      a . Proverbs 31:10 Literally a wife of valor, in the sense of all forms of excellence
